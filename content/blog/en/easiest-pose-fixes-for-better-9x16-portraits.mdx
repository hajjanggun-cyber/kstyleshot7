## Intro

Vertical portraits look awkward fast when the pose is too stiff or too compressed.

## Why This Matters

The 9:16 frame exaggerates posture, shoulder tension, and spacing more than people expect.

## 4 Easy Fixes

### 1. Relax the shoulders first

Tension in the shoulders makes the whole upper body look harder and less natural.

### 2. Leave a little space around the torso

A small amount of breathing room helps the frame feel cleaner.

### 3. Do not lock the chin

A slight release in the jaw and chin usually makes the portrait feel less rigid.

### 4. Keep the body stable, not frozen

You want control, not stiffness.

## Common Mistakes

- Squaring the shoulders too hard
- Cropping too tightly
- Holding tension in the neck and jaw

## Quick Checklist

- Relax shoulders
- Keep some framing room
- Soften the chin
- Stay stable, not stiff
