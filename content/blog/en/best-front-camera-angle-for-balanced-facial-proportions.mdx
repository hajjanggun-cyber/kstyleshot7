## Intro

The best front camera angle is usually less dramatic than people think.

## Why This Matters

Even a small angle shift changes the way the nose, jaw, eyes, and forehead appear in a front-facing portrait.

## 4 Practical Tips

### 1. Keep the lens just above eye level

This is usually the safest position for a balanced face shape without exaggerating the chin.

### 2. Tilt the phone only slightly

A gentle adjustment helps. A strong downward tilt often distorts the upper face.

### 3. Stay centered, not twisted

Turning one shoulder is fine, but the face should remain close to the lens center if you want a clean baseline.

### 4. Step back before cropping

Distance matters. It is better to stand slightly farther away and crop later than to press the camera too close.

## Common Mistakes

- Holding the phone too high
- Shooting from below the chin
- Letting the face drift to the frame edge

## Quick Checklist

- Lens slightly above eye level
- Minimal tilt
- Face near center
- Enough distance from the phone
