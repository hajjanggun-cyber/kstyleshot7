## Intro

The quality of the input selfie changes the final result more than most people expect.

## Why This Matters

If the source image is dark, blurry, tilted, or cluttered, the final portrait usually feels less polished.

## 3 Practical Tips

### 1. Face the camera directly

Keep your face centered and avoid a strong side angle for the cleanest baseline.

### 2. Use soft front lighting

Window light from the front is usually more forgiving than overhead indoor light.

### 3. Keep the background simple

A clean wall or calm room helps the face read better and reduces visual noise.

## Common Mistakes

- Using heavy beauty filters before upload
- Taking a selfie too close to the lens
- Shooting in dim yellow indoor light

## Quick Checklist

- Face centered
- Eyes clearly visible
- Soft front light
- Minimal background clutter
