## Intro

If your selfie feels off even before styling, the camera may simply be too close.

## Why This Matters

Short distance exaggerates facial features. That distortion carries into the upload and can make generated results less balanced.

## 4 Practical Tips

### 1. Give the lens some space

A little extra distance usually creates a more natural face shape than an arm-length close-up pushed too near.

### 2. Crop after the shot

Do not force the framing by moving the phone too close. Step back and trim the image later if needed.

### 3. Keep the face large enough to read

Distance helps, but the face still needs enough detail for the tool to read the eyes, hairline, and jaw.

### 4. Stay consistent across retakes

Using a similar distance every time makes it easier to compare which changes actually improved the result.

## Common Mistakes

- Extreme close-up selfies
- Wide-angle distortion from a near lens
- Inconsistent distance across multiple attempts

## Quick Checklist

- Avoid pushing the phone too close
- Keep facial detail clear
- Crop later if necessary
- Repeat with a consistent setup
