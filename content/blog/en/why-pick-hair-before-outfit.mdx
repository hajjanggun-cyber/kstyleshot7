## Intro

Many users want to start with clothing first, but that usually makes the rest of the flow harder.

## Why This Matters

Hair changes the face framing and overall mood faster than outfit does, so it gives the strongest early direction.

## 4 Practical Reasons

### 1. Hair sets the face mood first

Once the face framing feels right, it becomes much easier to judge which outfit fits the same energy.

### 2. Outfit reads better after the silhouette is stable

If hair still feels unsettled, outfit comparisons often become noisy and less useful.

### 3. It reduces random choices

Starting with one strong variable makes the whole selection flow feel less scattered.

### 4. It helps users compare results faster

Users usually notice facial balance before clothing details, especially in portrait crops.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting with outfit because it feels more familiar
- Changing multiple style directions at once
- Judging clothing before the face framing is settled

## Quick Checklist

- Choose one hair mood first
- Lock the face framing
- Compare outfits after that
- Keep the flow step by step
