## Intro

Soft layers are subtle, but they often change the emotional tone of a portrait faster than people expect.

## Why This Matters

When the camera sees more shape and movement in the hair, the whole image usually feels less flat.

## What Soft Layers Do

### 1. They create motion around the face

Even a slight change in edge shape can make the portrait feel lighter and more editorial.

### 2. They reduce visual heaviness

One-length silhouettes can read dense in front-facing selfies, especially under indoor lighting.

### 3. They help volume look more natural

Layers can make the hair look lifted without relying on stiff styling.

### 4. They pair well with simple makeup

If the hair already adds movement, the rest of the look can stay clean and minimal.

## Common Mistakes

- Cutting layers too aggressively for the target mood
- Leaving the crown flat
- Combining layers with overly busy framing

## Quick Checklist

- Keep the shape soft, not choppy
- Add movement near the cheek line
- Use front light to show texture
- Match the hair with a simple outfit
