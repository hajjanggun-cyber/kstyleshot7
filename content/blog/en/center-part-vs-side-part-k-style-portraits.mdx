## Intro

Parting choice looks small, but it changes the whole portrait balance.

## Why This Matters

Center and side parts shape the forehead, cheek framing, and symmetry in very different ways.

## Quick Comparison

### 1. Center part

Usually feels cleaner, calmer, and more symmetrical when the face and hairline are already balanced.

### 2. Side part

Often adds softness and movement when the portrait feels too flat or too centered.

### 3. When center part works better

It is usually safer when you want a sleek, stable editorial mood.

### 4. When side part works better

It can be better when you want more softness or less visual severity.

## Common Mistakes

- Picking a part line only because it feels trendy
- Ignoring how the camera angle affects the split
- Forcing symmetry when the photo already feels stiff

## Quick Checklist

- Want sleek? Try center
- Want softness? Try side
- Check the camera angle
- Compare before locking the choice
