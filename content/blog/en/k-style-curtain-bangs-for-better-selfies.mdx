## Intro

Curtain bangs are one of the easiest ways to soften a selfie without making the look feel overstyled.

## Why This Matters

The shape around the forehead and cheek line changes how the camera reads facial balance.

## 4 Practical Tips

### 1. Keep the center light

Curtain bangs work best when the center opens enough to keep the eyes visible.

### 2. Let the sides frame the cheeks

A gentle side sweep can make the face look cleaner without hiding too much structure.

### 3. Avoid a heavy, flat drop

If the fringe falls too dense and straight, the result can feel shorter and heavier on camera.

### 4. Pair it with a calm part line

Curtain bangs usually look best when the parting is stable and not fighting the fringe direction.

## Common Mistakes

- Making the center gap too narrow
- Leaving the ends blunt and stiff
- Using harsh overhead lighting that flattens the shape

## Quick Checklist

- Eyes visible
- Light opening at the center
- Soft cheek framing
- Front lighting, not top lighting
