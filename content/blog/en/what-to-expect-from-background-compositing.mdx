## Intro

Background compositing can improve the final mood a lot, but it is not magic.

## Why This Matters

Users who expect perfect cutout accuracy in every case are more likely to misread what the tool is designed to do.

## What to Expect

### 1. Simple backgrounds usually work best

Cleaner source images make separation and compositing more believable.

### 2. Complex edges can still look imperfect

Hair strands, busy clothing edges, and cluttered source backgrounds are the most common weak points.

### 3. Mood changes matter more than pixel perfection

The biggest value is often the atmosphere shift, not flawless edge detail under close inspection.

### 4. Better inputs still improve the final output

A stronger selfie and simpler styling choice give the composite a much better baseline.

## Common Mistakes

- Expecting studio-grade manual retouching
- Uploading cluttered source photos
- Zooming into edge details before judging the full portrait

## Quick Checklist

- Use a clean source selfie
- Keep expectations realistic
- Focus on overall mood first
- Avoid crowded original backgrounds
